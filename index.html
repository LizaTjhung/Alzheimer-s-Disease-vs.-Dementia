ALZHEIMERS DISEASE & DEMENTIA

ALZHEIMERS

    Definition:

  • Alzheimer's disease is a brain disorder that slowly destroys memory and thinking skills, and eventually, the ability to carry out the simplest tasks.

  • It is the most common cause of dementia.

      • Symptoms

    • Disruptive Memory loss. As the condition progresses, forgetting the simplest of things may become more and more frequent.
    • Difficulty Planning
    • Difficulty Completing Tasks
    • Confusion with Time or Place
    • Difficulty Understanding Image and Time
    • Problems Speaking
    • Decreased Judgment
    • Changes in Mood
    • Hesitation to Try New Things
    • Withdrawal from Social Activities

      Treatment

    • Alzheimer's medicines can help with memory symptoms and other cognitive changes. Two types of medicines used to treat symptoms include:

      1. Cholinesterase inhibitors. These medicines work by boosting levels of cell-to-cell communication. The medicines preserve a chemical messenger that is depleted in the brain by Alzheimer's disease.

        These are usually the first medicines tried, and most people see modest improvements in symptoms.
      2. Memantine (Namenda). This medicine works in another brain cell communication network and slows the progression of symptoms with moderate to severe Alzheimer's disease. It's sometimes used in combination with a cholinesterase inhibitor. Relatively rare side effects include dizziness and confusion.

      • Other medicines have been approved by the Food and Drug Administration, also called FDA, to slow declines in thinking and functioning caused by Alzheimer's disease. The medicines prevent amyloid plaques in the brain from clumping. They're prescribed for people with mild Alzheimer's disease and mild cognitive impairment due to Alzheimer's disease. These medicines include:

      1. Lecanemab-irmb (Leqembi). This medicine is given as an IV infusion every two weeks. Side effects may include infusion-related reactions such as fever, flu-like symptoms, nausea, vomiting, dizziness, changes in heart rate and trouble breathing.
      2. Donanemab-azbt (Kisunla). This medicine is given as an IV infusion every four weeks. Side effects may include flu-like symptoms, nausea, vomiting, headache, trouble breathing and changes in blood pressure.

DEMENTIA

    Definition:

  • Dementia is a general term for loss of memory, language, problem-solving and other thinking abilities that are severe enough to interfere with daily life.

  • It including Alzheimer's disease.

      • Symptoms

    • Memory loss: This can include forgetting recent conversations or events, or struggling to remember familiar people or places.
    • Difficulty with communication: This may involve struggling to find the right words, using inappropriate words, or having difficulty following conversations.
    • Struggling with decision-making and problem-solving: This may include difficulty with tasks that were previously easy, such as cooking or paying bills.
    • Changes in personality and behavior: This may involve becoming more withdrawn, agitated, or exhibiting inappropriate behaviors.
    • Struggling with spatial awareness: This may involve getting lost in familiar places or having difficulty with basic tasks such as dressing oneself.
    • Loss of motivation: This may involve a lack of interest in hobbies or activities that were previously enjoyed.
    • Difficulty with daily activities: This may include struggling with basic tasks such as bathing or brushing oneself.

        • Causes

        Dementia is caused by a variety of diseases that cause damage to brain cells. This damage interferes with the ability of brain cells to communicate with each other. When brain cells cannot communicate normally, thinking, behavior and feelings can be affected.

      • Alzheimer's disease, high levels of certain proteins inside and outside brain cells make it hard for brain cells to stay healthy and to communicate with each other.
      • Depression.
      • Medication side effects.
      • Excess use of alcohol.
      • Thyroid problems.
      • Vitamin deficiencies.

          • Treatment


        1. Cognitive behavioral therapy: This type of therapy can help individuals with dementia learn strategies to better manage their symptoms and improve their quality of life.
        2. Occupational therapy: This type of therapy can help individuals with dementia maintain their independence and improve their ability to perform daily activities.
        3. Supportive care: This may involve providing assistance with daily activities, managing behavior and mood changes, and providing support for caregivers.
        4. Nutrition and exercise: A healthy diet and regular physical activity can help improve cognitive function and slow the progression of dementia.
